People are free to enter the building. People inside the building need to remain there, with doors and windows closed. Air intake fans should be turned off if the outside air poses a health or safety risk for occupants. Normal operations may continue inside the building. Action Responsibility 1. Initiating a Shelter in Place If there is an emergency situation where a Shelter in Place is required, PSS management direction will be given by PSS management. Shelter in Place activation will be initiated by the Security team at the affected campus. Depending upon the emergency, PSS management will act on advice from Police or other emergency service agencies, as it pertains to the situation. 1.1 Campus Security dispatch will implement Shelter in Place by using the Campus Security ENS to announce Shelter in Place within the affected campus or building, by all text and speech methods currently available within the ENS system, including telephones, speakers, and digital signage.  Provide calm and informative announcement of the status and directions. 1.2 Campus Security will check to ensure that all exterior doors and Campus Security windows are closed. Where possible, all exterior doors will have a security member or designated warden stationed nearby, to advise community members that it is not safe to leave the building. 1.3 Air intake fans should be turned off to prevent outside air from Campus Facilities entering the building if it is determined that the air intake could pose a health or safety risk for occupants. 1.4 Management of PSS will then be notified by Campus Security of the Campus Security Shelter in Place status and given all information pertaining to the event and action taken. 1.5 The Vice President, Corporate Services and the Vice President, PSS management Marketing, Student Life & Alumni will be notified by a member of the PSS team with a full accounting of why the Shelter in Place was called and what actions have taken place. This may result in the launch of the Emergency Plan, and opening of the EOC, as well as initiating the Crisis Communication Plan. 11 Action Responsibility 2.
